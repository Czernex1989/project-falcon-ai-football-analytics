The second half became much more balanced. Both teams created chances, but neither side could find the winning goal until the closing minutes.

# 🦅 Match Story

Spain started the match with confidence and controlled the opening thirty minutes. After an excellent save from Thibaut Courtois, Fabián Ruiz reacted first to score from the rebound, giving Spain a deserved lead.

Belgium refused to give up. The team gradually found its rhythm, and Charles De Ketelaere equalised before half-time, ending Unai Simón's historic World Cup clean-sheet streak.

The second half was balanced, with both teams searching for the winning goal. The biggest turning point came when Thibaut Courtois left the pitch injured around the 70th minute. A few minutes later Kevin De Bruyne also had to leave the field.

Belgium appeared to lose confidence after losing two experienced leaders. Spain remained patient and continued to attack.

In the closing minutes, substitute goalkeeper Senne Lammens failed to control a long-range shot, allowing Mikel Merino to score the winning goal from close range.

Belgium players left the pitch in tears after a painful defeat, while Spain celebrated an emotional victory and a place in the FIFA World Cup semi-final against France.
