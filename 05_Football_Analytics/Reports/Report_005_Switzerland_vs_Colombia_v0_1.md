# 1. Match Context

Switzerland and Colombia met in the Round of 16.

The match was decided after a penalty shootout.

Switzerland advanced to the quarter-finals.

Both teams played with discipline and caution.

This report is based on match observation.

---

# 2. General Match Overview

The match finished 0-0 after normal time and extra time.

Both teams had problems creating clear chances.

Switzerland stayed compact and organised.

Colombia tried to play with energy and movement.

The game became a battle of patience and concentration.

Neither team wanted to take too many risks.

The penalty shootout decided the winner.

Switzerland showed more composure in the final moment.

---

# 3. Switzerland Analysis

Switzerland defended with good structure.

The team stayed compact for long periods.

Midfield players protected central areas.

The defensive line remained calm under pressure.

Switzerland avoided unnecessary risks.

The team tried to build attacks with patience.

Progression into the final third was difficult.

Switzerland looked more focused during key defensive moments.

The penalty shootout showed strong mentality.

---

# 4. Colombia Analysis

Colombia played with good intensity.

The team tried to move the ball quickly.

Wide areas were used to create attacking options.

Colombia looked dangerous in some transition moments.

However, clear chances were difficult to create.

The final pass was often missing.

Colombia competed well throughout the match.

The penalty shootout decided against them.

---

# 5. Attacking Phase

### Switzerland

Switzerland attacked with patience.

The team tried to avoid losing the ball in dangerous areas.

Build-up was controlled but sometimes slow.

More creativity was needed near the penalty area.

### Colombia

Colombia attacked with more speed.

The team tried to use movement between the lines.

Wide players helped stretch Switzerland's defence.

The final actions lacked precision.

---

# 6. Defensive Phase

### Switzerland

Switzerland defended with discipline.

Central spaces were protected well.

Players stayed close to each other.

Colombia had little space in dangerous zones.

### Colombia

Colombia also defended with good commitment.

The team pressed in selected moments.

Switzerland struggled to find easy passing lanes.

The defensive work kept the match balanced.

---

# 7. Transitions

Transitions were important but not always effective.

Colombia looked dangerous after recoveries.

Switzerland reacted quickly after losing possession.

Both teams controlled many transition moments before they became dangerous.

This helped keep the score at 0-0.

---

# 8. Key Tactical Battle

The key tactical battle was Colombia's energy against Switzerland's compact structure.

Colombia wanted to create movement and speed.

Switzerland wanted to slow the rhythm.

Switzerland managed this battle well.

---

# 9. Key Moment

The penalty shootout was the decisive moment.

Switzerland kept better focus under pressure.

Colombia lost the match despite a competitive performance.

The mental side became more important than open-play quality.
