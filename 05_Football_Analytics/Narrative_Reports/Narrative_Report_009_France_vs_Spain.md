# 2. Match Context

A place in the FIFA World Cup Final was at stake.

France entered the match hoping to extend Didier Deschamps' remarkable international career with one more final appearance.

Spain arrived after another disciplined performance against Belgium and looked increasingly confident with every knockout match.

Both teams selected a 4-2-3-1 formation, suggesting that tactical discipline would become more important than individual brilliance.

The match promised small margins.

It delivered exactly that.

Spain controlled possession slightly better, but France remained compact and patient without the ball.

The game changed in the twentieth minute.

Lucas Digne fouled inside the penalty area.

Spain converted the penalty and immediately forced France into a different match than they had planned.

Instead of waiting for transitions, France now had to chase the result.

Only a few minutes later another important moment followed.

William Saliba was forced to leave the pitch because of injury.

The defensive partnership changed much earlier than Didier Deschamps had expected.

Although France continued to compete, the defensive organisation no longer looked as comfortable.

Spain recognised that immediately.

Their circulation became quicker.

Their movement between the lines became more confident.

France still enjoyed long periods with the ball.

However, possession rarely became genuine danger.

The second half followed a similar pattern.

Didier Deschamps replaced Adrien Rabiot after his difficult first half, influenced by an early yellow card and another strong challenge before the break.

France tried to increase the tempo through attacking substitutions.

Spain answered in the best possible way.

Pedro Porro doubled the lead and almost decided the semi-final.

From that moment Spain controlled not only the scoreboard but also the emotional rhythm of the match.

# 4. Team Analysis

## France

France started with a clear defensive structure and tried to remain compact without the ball.

For long periods the organisation looked solid.

However, after conceding the opening goal, the team struggled to change the rhythm of the match.

The injury to Saliba forced an early defensive adjustment that reduced stability at the back.

Rabiot's yellow card also limited France's aggression in midfield before he was replaced at half-time.

Several attacking substitutions were introduced during the second half, but none of them significantly changed the flow of the game.

France finished with ten shots but failed to create a single big chance.

The team showed commitment until the final whistle but lacked quality in decisive moments.

---

## Spain

Spain approached the match with patience and confidence.

The team never rushed their attacks and waited for the right opportunities.

After taking the lead, Spain controlled both possession and tempo.

Their midfield remained calm under pressure and constantly offered passing options.

The defensive line stayed compact and rarely allowed France to attack central spaces.

Spain also managed the final stages intelligently by introducing fresh players without changing the tactical balance.

It was a mature performance from the first minute until the last.

# 5. Attacking Phase

Spain proved far more efficient in the final third.

The opening goal forced France to abandon their original game plan.

Spain continued to create the better chances and scored a second goal through Pedro Porro early in the second half.

Although both teams finished with the same number of shots, Spain's opportunities carried much greater quality.

France struggled to move the ball into dangerous areas and often finished attacks with low-probability attempts.

---

# 6. Defensive Phase

Spain defended with discipline throughout the match.

The back line remained well organised and protected the central areas effectively.

France enjoyed periods of possession but rarely found space behind the Spanish defence.

Mbappé spent large parts of the match away from dangerous central positions.

Even seven French corner kicks failed to produce a clear scoring opportunity.

Spain's defensive organisation remained one of the biggest differences between the two teams.

---

# 7. Transitions

Spain reacted quickly after winning possession.

Several dangerous attacks started immediately after French mistakes.

France attempted to attack with greater urgency after falling behind, but transitions often ended too early because of inaccurate final passes.

Spain remained balanced in every phase of the game.

---

# 8. Key Tactical Battle

The battle in midfield decided the match.

Spain controlled the rhythm without dominating possession.

France circulated the ball well but rarely progressed into dangerous areas.

Spain's midfield constantly disrupted French attacks and prevented quick combinations around the penalty area.

The early yellow card for Rabiot and Saliba's injury gradually shifted the tactical balance towards Spain.

---

# 9. Key Moment

The penalty in the twentieth minute changed everything.

Before that moment the match remained balanced.

After Spain took the lead, France were forced to attack more openly.

The second goal by Pedro Porro almost ended the contest.

From that point Spain focused on controlling the match rather than chasing another goal.
